Fejér’s proof of a minimum property of the orthic triangle
by Marius Kempe
2015

“[Fejér] could perceive the significance, the beauty, and the promise of a rather concrete, not too large problem […] and, when he had found the solution, he kept on working at it with loving care, till each detail became fully intuitive and the connection of the details in a well-ordered whole fully transparent. It is due to such care spent on the elaboration of the solution that […] most of his proofs appear very clear and simple.”

Proof.

The epigraph is from Pólya’s obituary of Fejér.
The visual approach is inspired by Bill Casselman’s review of Tufte’s Visual Explanations.
The proof was first published in chapter 6 of The Enjoyment of Mathematics by Rademacher & Toeplitz.
The problem was first posed and solved by G. F. Fagnano in Nova Acta Eruditorum (1775): 281-303.
